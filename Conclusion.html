That's a pretty important factor when evaluating their performance, because according to their coach, they are considered valuable pieces in their team for the purpose of winning. It's not just comparing an actual starter against an end of the bench guy, because they both do start. But it's obvious that their roles are different. Take Kon Knueppel for example. He has an average separation factor of 8.48, but that number is mostly flawed due to the places in which he receives the ball. Sometimes, he gets the ball in the back court and helps to run plays, and sometimes he spots up as a shooter. This can be measured in the range of the data tracking the separation between him and his defender, which is a large number of 49 ft. That means that Kon received the ball within a data set that included 49 different numbers, meaning that he received the ball from various places around the floor. With Ace, however, he wasn't as used on offense as much as Kon. He created 6.83 ft of separation for himself as well, but that was due to him receiving the ball mostly outside the three point line, where defense and coverage is usually consistently softer, leading to higher spacing numbers. This shows a wide difference in between their play styles and team structure, especially due to the fact that both players were drafted for these specific purposes. Kon was drafted out of college as a player with remarkable shooting upside, and fits within the Hornets structure as someone that can consistently knock down shots and give the man ball handlers some safety when passing. Ace Bailey is considered a very raw prospect, as he possesses a lot of important tools to be a star NBA player, but he's very inconsistent, and doesn't really have a lot of control. This is also evident when watching both teams play without these players. With Kon Knueppel, when he left the court, the ball handlers were left without one of their main options, forcing them to have a heavier load, and even pushing the pace a little bit. This lead to a stretch where the Hornets turned the ball over 3 times in 2 minutes. Their defense was also a lot less stable. But it's also evident when he's off the floor, as when he leaves, the Hornets perimeter defense somehow collapse, perhaps due to a large hole being created on the inside when Kon leaves, which allowed the Raptors to take many threes, and even make most of them. Though Kon's BPM (-4)compared to the rest of the team's BPM (-1), it was obvious that it was a product of the situation as well, as when Kon left in the second quarter, the Raptors were in the bonus, leading to a lot more free throws for the Hornets, definitely impacting their BPM by a lot.

This, along with his limited role as a spacer within George and Markennan's two man game caused the minutes without him to be very much unchanged. I mean, they used Kevin Love a lot more on actions than they do with Bailey. It makes sense, as the Jazz are still trying to find their identity as a team, and I expect that Bailey's role will change eventually during the season, but it honestly seems kind of tragic how little he produces on the team.
